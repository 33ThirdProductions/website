Tridosha:
The Warrior Within

Draft script/ideas for the preview montage:
I'm thinking start with scenes of children running and playing together. Very sincere, no corny forshadowing, but we're clearly seeign better times. Everything in slow motion, with the scenes fading in and out, as if in the minds eye. This is memory. We get glimpes... like worried parents hushing themselves, scenes with clear double meanings... like pretending that running to the basement for an air raid call is because "Wouldn't it be fun to go camping tonight?" Could go "war" or just "poor". I actually don't have a clear sense of what time to set this. Could be future sci-fi-ish (sowewhat the aesthetic of "Chronicles of Ridick"). Future-kung fu is awesome. Or we could do some alternate or golden history type thing. Set in some kind of "imperial time" (though could work with many cultures as wel...). Or even place it in the present. "Near term sci-fi." An answer to the "post acopalypse" genre of the late 70s and eighties and nineties... that one may be my favorite.
"Two brothers, grew up as children at a time when many were forced to become adults too soon. But even the unquestioning love of their parents was not enough to shield them entirely..."
Fade to silence as we do a few "heart-beat-cut-to-black-flashes" montages that imply that the parent's get killed. Either violence, or maybe just stress. We catch words here and there to get an idea. Would it be too corny to have a heart monitor going over this? Obviously this is all over the top, but I really want to play it sincere.
Now the void between the cuts starts to shorten. As we move up to the present and where the main action of the movie really starts. In a theatrical release, I could see the history playing out during the opening credits. In a comic book, as a "Prologue". I like this because if we spend about half the preview on this, we'll still have a real draw, but we don't have to give much of the movie away. (This next bit is still memory, just recent memory, clearer, sharper...)
"One brother vows to destroy the evils of this world whatever it takes... swallowing his compassion and hardening his heart, he trades his soul to become an avenger."
Scenes of meetings, purchasing weapons, some kind of battle or bank robbery or whatever... in an anti-apocalypse, maybe he's something like the guy in Fight Club? But not yet restrained, definetly resorting to violence, though it's clearly hard. In the back story, it would be very few deaths, I still think he's careful, but in the movie, we focus on a few moments... a comarade trips, we see or hear him getting killed as the brother tears himself away to escape a hopeless situation, a bystander accidentally gets caught in the crossfire. These weigh heavy on the brother's mind.
Different voice, implicitly from the other brother: "It wasn't just my parents who died that day. My brother... he's dying as well..."
We come up as if someone with their head bowed and eyes closed (implicitly, it's the younger brother who's memorie's we've been seeing, raising his head and looking at the master.) "Teach me to save him." (Would it be too much to have faint heart beating and breathing the entire time that we don't even notice at first, but which gets louder and louder until it cuts off and we hear an intake of breath to speak those words?
That's where the movie begins, so now we do fast cut montages. Back to the voiceover guy. We focus mainly on training scenes with a few of the first action scenes (someone attacks the dojo?) as the climax of the preview. (So the last scene we see is within the first 15-30 min of the movie.)
"One man who's struggle to save his brother from a life of violence while still walking the path of peace leads him to a master..."
Voice of master: "You must have confidence in this choice..." (Backstory: the warrior monks will teach you to fight, but you must foreswear violence entirely. I want some of the same feeling I get when I think of the Marathon Monks in Japan... I mean, that's commitment.)
master: "You must be willing to die..."
younger brother: "I forgive you..."
master: "And should you die, I vow to commit myself to a life of penance." (actually, this would be in the movie, but let's cut from the preview).
The master then brutally punches the student, sending him flying across the room. Maybe like a slow-mo kick that goes full speed at the moment of impact, sending the student across the room. (All that wolud be one 10-15 sec. sequence.
Training montage. Break and focus:
master: "First you must learn to fuse your weaker constitutions. The fire in you will not yield yet..."
Let next scene roll:
announcer: "He will seak the key to ultimate physical control"
master: "To fuse all three constitutions simultaneously... my master achieved it once..."
brother: "So it can be done?"
master: "It was a moment of sacrifice."
brother: "I wish to make each moment a sacrifice to my desire to free him..."
master: "Then let us begin."
Cresendo and white out; fade back to title: "Tridosha: The Warrior Within"
