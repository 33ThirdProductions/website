Volume I: The Corruption

Overview Notes

  • Noah and his family live far outside the city/corrupted area. A day or two travel to the city is ideal.
  • In some way, Noah as been forced to reveal that he can speak with God (or can he?). Perhaps he has diverted some disaster, or saved some people from a disaster, something along these lines. This can be played out in the text, or can simply be referenced as a past event.
  • The corrupt in power (perhaps a low-level “boss” type) has learned of Noah's supposed ability. He wishes to use this to his advantage, to foresee his own destiny or perhaps to plan a coup of some sort.
  • The “Boss” sends a lieutenant and a group of mercenaries to kidnap Noah. Their instructions are to either bring Noah back alive or take a member of his family and leave behind a ransom note.
  • Noah and his sons are warned of the attack just prior to the kidnappers arrival. This may be in the form of a vision, or perhaps something much more simple. At this point, it should probably not be made implicit to the reader exactly what has occurred, only that they have been warned. It may have been divine, it may have just been luck, or Noah may just be that vigilant.
  • Noah and his sons fight off and kill the intruders, but a wife is taken hostage. Probably Noah's wife. Perhaps the intruders plan from the start was to send men to be slaughtered by Noah and sons as a distraction so that the lieutenant can get away with the wife. This works well because it shows that the corrupt do not value human life whatsoever, and gladly sacrifice their own men to gain what they want.
  • Japeth, being the wisest, sets out in pursuit. He trails the intruders but stays hidden as he is still outnumbered. That night, Japeth sends a messenger such as a nomadic hunter to Noah with news that the intruders have entered the city. The city is walled, but people can freely come and go as they please.
  • Noah and Ham set out to meet Japeth. Shem, being fiercely loyal and taking pride in doing whatever duty is asked of him, stays behind with the remaining wives. Noah and Ham travel to the city, perhaps meeting with some adversity or a small combat scene along the way.
  • Japeth reaches the city first and devises a plan, which is unknown to Noah or the reader. Japeth allows himself to be taken prisoner in lieu of Noah, who he claims is old and travels slowly. Japeth then sends word through a child or thief to Noah and Ham to meet him at a third location.
  • Japeth bribes a guard to release himself and the wife. They flee and go to meet Noah and Ham at the third location. The guard has double-crossed Japeth and led the corrupt straight to the party members. But Japeth knew this would happen, and has set up the third location for a quick get away. Perhaps Japeth even sends a coded message with the child or thief to give Noah a “heads up” about what will be going down at the third meeting spot. A message that only Noah will understand, and can possibly be referenced earlier in the text to let the reader in on the secret as well. Noah may not know exactly what is going to take place, but he can garner just enough info from the message, knowing how Japeth thinks, to be prepared for what comes.
  • Here a confrontation between the corrupted and Noah takes place, where they discuss his ability to talk to God. Then Japeth sets his plan in motion, and the party is able to escape.
