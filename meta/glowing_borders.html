Overview

The new CSS3 'border-image' is great for custom borders. To get it working takes a little bit of planning and careful image setup. But once properly done, it's relatively robust.

Limitations

Without the CSS3 image border, there's no clean way to achive this effect. this kind of effect requires introducing extra elements to contain the border images. It's not pretty and it's and fragile.
The non-CSS3 solutions are also incompatible with the CSS3 solution. Non-CSS image borders require a bunch of tables or divs... they break the idea of CSS. So make a graceful standard border fallback and tell users to upgrade if they want the image borders.

Border Image Basics

You can check out the W3 specs or plenty of places for the boring details. The basic idea is to take an image and define 4 inset cut lines that divide the image into four coners, a top, right, bottom, and left border, and the center image. Use the handy tool to wrap your head around it.
